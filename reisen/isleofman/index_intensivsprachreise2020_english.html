Intensiv-Sprachreise 2020

In the footsteps of the Celts and Vikings: Journey to the Isle of Man – A journey that will boost your English!

Rugged coastline, medieval castles and rural landscape, rising to a mountainous center


8-Day Journey to the Isle of Man: 18.07.–25.07.2020

Saturday, 18 July
Departure from your home in the early morning
Journey to Rotterdam
If there is spare time, we will have a beach walk along the famous Scheveningen beach before boarding our ship
Overnight Channel crossing from Rotterdam to Hull including dinner and overnight stay in a cabin on the ferry as well as breakfast

Sunday, 19 July
After disembarking, we will go to the medieval town of York to have a guided tour of the city and the famous cathedral.
After a short lunchbreak, we will continue our tour to Liverpool where a local guide will show us all the highlights of the city including the Beatles mystery, as well as inform us about the hard times of the Blitz war and the resurrection of the town after World War Two.
In the late afternoon we will embark the ferry to the Isle of Man and cross the Irish Sea to Douglas. There will be a chance to grab some dinner on the ferry.
For our next overnight stays, we will be accommodated at a ***hotel in Ballasalla.

Monday, 20 July
Today we will explore Churchtown. We will visit the local secondary school and shadow some teachers in their classes there. After a talk about the educational system on the Isle of Man and in the United Kingdom, we will go on a tour of the city – guided by students of the school. They will show us Castle Rushen, the old Royal residence, and all the other highlights of their home town.
In the evening we will have individual and personal talks with some teachers from the school in a local pub.
Then we will return to our hotel in Ballasalla.

Tuesday, 21 July
After breakfast, we will return to the school in Churchtown to have an assembly discussion about the German and English educational systems. We will learn some more details about challenges of the British school system and present our German educational scheme to the students and the teachers there.
In the afternoon we will go to Port St. Mary and take a walk around Calf Sound – a scenic region along the cliffs of the Isle of Man.

Wednesday, 22 July
After breakfast we will go to Port Erin and enjoy the sea view in this little town.
Then we will take a boat and go to the island Calf of Man. During this boat trip we can see seals, puffins, and other maritime inhabitants, and perhaps even a shark basking in the sun.
In the afternoon we will go to Peel and visit the picturesque Peel Castle which is located on a little peninsula off the town of Peel.
Our last stop for the day will be in Kirk Michael before we return to our hotel in Ballasalla.

Thursday, 23 July
We check out of our hotel after breakfast and travel to Laxey. Here, we board an electric train to the top of Snaefell, the highest hill on the Isle of Man. In fine weather, we can see Ireland as well as England and even Scotland from the hilltop.
In the afternoon we go back to Douglas to catch the ferry back to the English mainland.
For our next overnight stay, we go on to Leeds.

Friday, 24 July
After a hearty breakfast at our hotel we go on a guided tour of the city of Leeds. Especially the museum “Royal Armouries” will impress us with its various kinds of weapons from different periods of human history.
In the evening we will catch the ferry from Hull to Rotterdam, where we will enjoy a nice dinner, entertainment and a cabin for overnight stay.

Saturday, 25 July
An intensive but very interesting and inspiring week lies behind us. After breakfast on the ship we will return to our homes.
